What’s the difference between the RAKE and NO RAKE charts?

The NO RAKE charts assume $0 is taken out of the pot after the flop. These charts are best used* for games with timed rake or very low rake.

The RAKE charts assume 5% is taken out of the pot, capped at 2bb, which is similar to the rake in most $1/$2 through $2/$5 games. These charts are best used* if you’re playing $2/$5 or below.

*As Nick says in the course, these charts are meant to give you an idea of the general shape of the strategy for each situation – not necessarily to be followed exactly. 

Since these are solver-generated charts, they assume your opponents will have the perfect equilibrium responses to each of your actions. In practice, your opponents will not play a perfect equilibrium strategy and thus you should deviate from the charts in the ways Nick explains throughout the course.

What do the abbreviations mean?

EP - Early Position
MP - Middle Position 
HJ - Hijack
CO - Cutoff
BTN or BU - Button
SB - Small Blind
BB - Big Blind

SQZ - Squeeze (3-betting after a raise and at least one call)
STR - Straddle
100str - 100 straddles deep (e.g. if the straddle is $10 and you have $1,000, you are 100 straddles deep)
ISO - Raising over a limp
